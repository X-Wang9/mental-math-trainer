Core Calculation Methods

Addition

Make-a-round-number: Push a number close to a ten or hundred first, then adjust back the difference.

38 + 47 = 38 + 50 - 3 = 88 - 3 = 85

Stepwise method: Split by place value and add from high place to low place.

386 + 247: 300+200=500, 80+40=120, 6+7=13 -> 633

Subtraction

Complement method: Treat the subtrahend as a nearby round number, subtract the round number, then adjust back.

735 - 298 = 735 - 300 + 2 = 437

Splitting: Convert subtraction into familiar operations.

17 - 9 = 17 - 10 + 1 = 8

Multiplication

Distributive law: Split one factor into a round ten plus a tail.

16 x 7 = 10x7 + 6x7 = 70 + 42 = 112

Mirror method: Same tens and complementary ones: front digit x (front digit + 1), then append ones x ones.

34 x 36: 3x4=12, 4x6=24 -> 1224

Division

Estimate quotient: Start from the high place, estimate step by step, and verify.

648 / 6: 600/6=100, 48/6=8 -> 108

Division with remainder: First find the largest integer quotient, then remainder = dividend - quotient x divisor.

697 / 8: 8x87=696, 697-696=1 -> quotient 87 remainder 1

Tips for Memorizing the 10-20 Table

Multiples of 11: 11xn = nx10 + n, meaning add n after 10n.

11x12 = 120+12 = 132 | 11x15 = 150+15 = 165

Multiples of 12: 12xn = 10xn + 2xn. Calculate the ten-times part first, then add double.

12x13 = 130+26 = 156 | 12x16 = 160+32 = 192

Multiples of 15: 15xn = 10xn + 5xn = 10xn + nx10/2

15x14 = 140+70 = 210 | 15x18 = 180+90 = 270

Squares: 11²=121, 12²=144, 13²=169, 14²=196, 15²=225, 16²=256, 17²=289, 18²=324, 19²=361, 20²=400

Symmetry: axb = bxa, so you only need to remember half.

13x17 = 17x13 = 221; remember just one direction.

Numbers near 20: 19xn = 20xn - n, and 18xn = 20xn - 2xn.

19x15 = 300-15 = 285 | 18x14 = 280-28 = 252

Shortcut Collection

Audio-Image Addition

Idea: Build a sound image of the numbers in your head, fix hundreds, tens, and ones by place, then merge from high place to low place.

• Read the number clearly and fix each digit’s position in your head.

• Merge from the high place. Carries should immediately land in the matching position.

• Do not stare only at the ones; do not let low-place calculation crowd out high-place memory.

386 + 247: 300+200=500, 80+40=120, 6+7=13 -> 633

4386 + 2747: 4300+2700=7000, 86+47=133 -> 7133

Complement Subtraction

Idea: Calculate from high place to low place using complements to avoid the mess of repeated borrowing.

• First place: first digit of minuend - (first digit of subtrahend + 1)

• Middle places: minuend up to that place + the 9-complement of the corresponding subtrahend digit

• Ones: remaining minuend + the 10-complement of the subtrahend ones digit

343 - 191: first 3-(1+1)=1, then 14+(9-complement of 9 is 0)=14, finally 143+(10-complement of 1 is 9)=152

735 - 298: first 7-(2+1)=4, then 73+(9-complement of 9 is 0)=73, finally 735+(10-complement of 8 is 2)=437

6240 - 1996: first 6-(1+1)=4, then 62+(9-complement of 9 is 0)=62, 624+(9-complement of 9 is 0)=624, finally 6240+(10-complement of 6 is 4)=4244

Mirror Multiplication

Idea: When two numbers have the same tens/front part and complementary ones/tail (sum to 10), use the shortcut formula.

• Front x (front+1) gives the first part.

• Tail x tail gives the second part; pad to two digits if needed.

• Join the two parts for the answer.

34 x 36: 3x4=12, 4x6=24 -> 1224

304 x 306: 30x31=930, 4x6=24 -> 93024

1204 x 1206: 120x121=14520, 4x6=24 -> 1452024

Leading Division

Idea: Exact division drill. Look first at the divisor and the leading digit of the quotient, then recover the exact result quickly.

• Only exact division problems are used; the answer must be an integer.

• Start from the high place of the dividend and estimate step by step.

• Verify by multiplication: quotient x divisor = dividend.

648 / 6: 600/6=100, 48/6=8 -> 108

5327 / 143: estimate quotient 37; 143x37=5291... adjust as needed

x11 Shortcut (Vedic Math)

Rule: First and last digits stay unchanged; write sums of adjacent digits in the middle, carrying when the sum reaches 10.

35 x 11 -> first 3, last 5, middle 3+5=8 -> 385

78 x 11 -> first 7, last 8, middle 7+8=15 with carry -> 858

234 x 11 -> first 2, last 4, middle 2+3=5 and 3+4=7 -> 2574

Squares Ending in 5

Rule: Remove the 5, multiply the remaining number by one more than itself, then append 25.

35² -> 3x4=12, append 25 -> 1225

65² -> 6x7=42, append 25 -> 4225

105² -> 10x11=110, append 25 -> 11025

Multiplication Near 100

Rule: Find each number’s difference from 100. Front = 100 minus the sum of the differences; tail = product of the differences, padded to two digits.

96 x 94 -> differences are -4 and -6; front 100-10=90; tail 4x6=24 -> 9024

103 x 97 -> differences +3 and -3; front 100+0=100; tail 3x3=09 -> 10009

Magic-Number Multiplication

Source: Zhang Chengqi, Magic-Number Multiplication.

11-24 squares: a=m+10; a²=(a+m)x10+m². When m² has more than one digit, it naturally merges and carries with the front.

17²: m=7, (17+7)x10+7²=240+49=289.

26-74 squares: magic number = A-50; A²=(25+magic number)x100+magic number². Negative magic numbers are still substituted directly; the square is positive.

67²: magic number 17, (25+17)x100+17²=4200+289=4489.

36²: magic number -14, (25-14)x100+(-14)²=1100+196=1296.

76-124 squares: magic number = A-100; A²=(A+magic number)x100+magic number². If the square has fewer than two digits, pad with 0; if more, carry naturally.

112²: magic number 12, (112+12)x100+12²=12400+144=12544.

84²: magic number -16, (84-16)x100+(-16)²=6800+256=7056.

All-purpose square method: m is the difference between A and a nearby round ten. A²=(A²-m²)+m²=(A-m)(A+m)+m².

28²: m=2, 28²=28²-2²+2²=(28-2)(28+2)+2²=26x30+4=784.

Multiplying any two numbers: Let a1<a2 and m=(a2-a1)/2. With the same parity, a1xa2=(a1+m)²-m². With one odd and one even, adjust one step to make same parity, then add back once.

36x49=35x49+49=(35+7)²-7²+49=42²-7²+49=1764.

Advanced arbitrary square: A=m1x100+m2; A²=m1x(A+m2)x100+m2².

234²: m1=2, m2=34, 2x(234+34)x100+34²=53600+1156=54756.

Magic Math in Daily Life: Supplement

Any square: Pick a convenient difference D. A²=(A+D)(A-D)+D².

314²=(314+14)x(314-14)+14²=328x300+196=98596.

Cube: A³=(A-D)xAx(A+D)+D²xA.

92³=90x92x94+2²x92=778688.

Multiplying two numbers: When close to a square, use midpoint square minus squared difference; near a round ten or hundred, shift both numbers and correct.

92x94=93²-1=8648; 92x94=90x96+2x4=8648.

Estimation: Round to thousands, hundreds, or tens first. For multiplication, add or subtract the same number from both factors to approach an easier base.

Vedic Math Multiplication

Centered around a round number: When a round ten, hundred, or thousand lies between A1 and A2, use the same square method: middle number squared minus difference squared.

17x23=20²-3²=400-9=391.

Near 100: When at least one factor is near 100, use the base-difference method: cross add/subtract for the front, multiply differences for the tail.

91x91=(91-9)x100+9x9=8281; 19x99=(19-1)x100+81x1=1881.

Number ending in 5 times an even number: Divide the even number by 2, 4, or 8; multiply the number ending in 5 by 2, 4, or 8; then multiply the two results.

22x15=(22/2)x(15x2)=11x30=330.

Divisibility, Decimals, and Magic Numbers

Divisibility: For 4, check whether the last two digits are divisible by 4. For 8, check the last three digits. For 3, the digit sum must be divisible by 3. For 9, the digit sum must be divisible by 9. For 6, the number must be even and its digit sum divisible by 3.

11 divisibility: From left to right, alternately subtract and add digits. The result should be 0 or a multiple of 11.

7 divisibility: Add or subtract multiples of 7 and see whether the simplified number is divisible by 7. This also works for other odd numbers not ending in 5.

Fractions to decimals: Learn patterns for denominators 2 through 11. Fractions with denominator 7 rotate around 142857.

1/7=0.142857..., 2/7=0.285714...

Modulo-9 error check: When the total-sum difference is 0 or negative, add 9 to the difference and compare again. Good for checking arithmetic.

Magic number: (A x 2 + 12) / 2 - A always equals 6. For a three-digit number, arrange digits high-to-low and low-to-high: ABC-CBA=DEF, then DEF+FED=1089.

Day of the week: month code + date + year code, then reduce by weekday cycle.

24 Game Tips

Core idea: Use basic anchors such as 3x8=24, 4x6=24, 12x2=24, and 15+9=24.

• First look for two numbers that can help make 24.

• Use division to create larger effects, such as 8/(1-2/3)=24.

• Use 1 flexibly: multiplying by 1 keeps a value unchanged; adding/subtracting 1 fine-tunes.

(1, 5, 5, 5) -> 5x(5-1/5) = 5x4.8 = 24

(3, 3, 8, 8) -> 8/(3-8/3) = 8/(1/3) = 24
